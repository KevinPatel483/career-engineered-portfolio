## Professional Direction
For this project, I chose the professional direction of Aspiring AI Product Engineer / Applied AI Engineer.

This direction connects my real IT support background with my future interest in software, databases, automation, and AI-powered tools. I want to build practical systems that help users solve real problems.

## Project Overview
This website is an aspirational professional portfolio. It represents the professional identity I am working toward.

The site includes:
- My professional direction
- My target audience
- My skills and tools
- Project proof
- Future project concepts
- My AI workflow
- My publishing process
- A swipe-card project section

The goal of this portfolio is not to pretend that I am already an expert. The goal is to show the direction I want to grow toward and the kind of work I want to be known for.

## Course Design System Influence
I studied the course design system before rebuilding my portfolio. I noticed that the course site used:
- A clear guided path
- Large professional headlines
- Rounded cards
- Soft background colors
- Small uppercase labels
- Process-based sections
- Proof-focused writing

I used those ideas to create a portfolio that feels intentional, organized, and professional.

## Research and Planning
Before building the final version, I thought about:
- Who the portfolio is for
- What professional direction I want to show
- What kind of work belongs in the portfolio
- What proof makes the site believable
- What style fits my professional identity
- How AI could help without replacing my own decisions

My audience is recruiters, IT teams, and people looking for junior technical talent with systems knowledge and interest in AI-powered tools.

## AI Workflow
I used AI tools such as ChatGPT and Gemini to help with:
- Researching professional roles
- Planning the portfolio structure
- Improving writing
- Generating project ideas
- Thinking about visual design
- Preparing my final presentation

I reviewed and revised the AI output so the final portfolio matched my own background, goals, and professional direction. I used AI as a workflow tool, not as a replacement for my own judgment.
